Chapter 3: Managing Knowledge in the Digital Enterprise

Capturing and leveraging organizational intelligence through digital systems, creating lasting competitive advantages.

1. Breaking the "Trapped Data" Mindset: The key hurdle is the belief that information is difficult to access. The chapter advocates that crucial data must be instantly available (often with "three clicks") to the people who need to make decisions.

2. Empowering the Knowledge Worker: It's not enough to have the technology; employees, particularly middle managers, must be taught how to analyze real-time data (financial reports, customer feedback, etc.) so they can make better and faster decisions.

3. Creating Institutional Intelligence: The DNS must integrate individual analytical skills into a cohesive collective knowledge. Meetings should be used for debate and decision-making, not for presenting data that could have been reviewed digitally beforehand.

4. The Drive Toward a Paperless Process: The standardization and digitization of all forms, linked to integrated digital workflows, are essential to eliminate slow manual processes, instantly track data, and facilitate innovation. partes que precisam dela, permitindo à empresa reagir na velocidade do pensamento.
