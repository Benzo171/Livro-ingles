Chapter 2: Commerce Redefined in the Digital Age

How the internet transforms business-customer relationships, creating friction-free markets and enabling global reach.

The Vision of Ubiquitous Digital Change Future technological changes, predicted to be widespread within a decade, will fundamentally alter business: Ubiquitous Computing and Connectivity: Computers and high-speed internet will be common in both the workplace and homes. Digital Everything: Communication (email), information flow (bills, bookings), and equipment (phones, cameras) will all become digital. Portable Data: Versatile digital notebooks will allow users to carry all personal and professional data. Preparation requires implementing a "digital nervous system" to handle three shifts: Digital Self-Service: Most interactions (B2C, B2B) will become digital and automated. High-Value Customer Service: Human involvement will shift to providing high-value, personalized advice. Internal Digitalization: Companies must adopt digital processes internally to remain adaptable. Executives are urged to personally use digital tools to understand this new world. The Rule of Value for Middlemen The Internet creates an efficient market where buyers and sellers connect directly, reducing the need for simple intermediaries. Disintermediation: Simple middlemen (those who only transfer goods or information) are rapidly losing value. The New Mandate: Intermediaries must add value (e.g., highly personalized services) or become high-volume, low-cost providers using self-service technology. Companies like Merrill Lynch exemplify this by investing in technology to empower their core assets (consultants) to provide higher-value advice. Embracing the Web Lifestyle Businesses must use the internet for creative customer interaction, focusing on online experience and service. Explosive Growth: Online sales are growing exponentially. Educated Customers: Companies find that informed customers are better customers, shifting the sales team's role to a consultant. Societal Shift: The "Web lifestyle" is enabling people to turn to the internet for news, entertainment, finance, and community, broadening horizons and strengthening cultural connections. Speed and Flexible Boundaries Digital information flow changes organizational structure and processes. Focus on Core Skills: Companies should outsource non-core functions to scale efficiently and focus on their core competencies. Virtual Companies: Small and medium businesses can partner digitally to act as large companies. Speed to Market: Digital organization is crucial for dramatically shortening the time it takes to get products and services to customers, a vital necessity for modern competitiveness. The ultimate message is that the Web lifestyle is arriving quickly and permanently, and businesses must act now to adopt digital tools and embrace the change to remain competitive.
